        <h2>Here are some of Katherine Johnson's accomplishments:
        </h2>

        <ul class="left" id="tribute-info">
          <li>Calculated trajectories for Project Mercury missions at NASA including Alan Shepard's first American spaceflight and John Glenn's orbital flight.</li>
          <li>Was specially requested to verify computer calculations by astronaut John Glenn.</li>
          <li>Used expertise in math to calculate trajectories, launch windows, and emergency return paths for spacecraft in the Apollo II mission and for the Space Shuttle.</li>
          <li>Overcame racial and gender barriers as a black woman working at NASA during a period of racial segregation.</li>
          <li>Influential and respected figure who inspired many to pursue careers in the STEM field.</li>
          <li>In 2016, NASA dedicated the Katherine G. Johnson Computational Building in honor of her groundbreaking work in space travel.</li>
          <li>Katherine was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom at the age of 97.</li>
          <li>Her story was highlighted in the book and successful movie "Hidden Figures".</li>
        </ul>

        <p class="quote">"Like what you do, and then you will do your best."</p>
