Katherine Johnson

The mathematician who made space travel possible

Here are some of Katherine Johnson's accomplishments:

  • Calculated trajectories for Project Mercury missions at NASA including Alan Shepard's first American spaceflight and John Glenn's orbital flight.
  • Was specially requested to verify computer calculations by astronaut John Glenn.
  • Used expertise in math to calculate trajectories, launch windows, and emergency return paths for spacecraft in the Apollo II mission and for the Space Shuttle.
  • Overcame racial and gender barriers as a black woman working at NASA during a period of racial segregation.
  • Influential and respected figure who inspired many to pursue careers in the STEM field.
  • In 2016, NASA dedicated the Katherine G. Johnson Computational Building in honor of her groundbreaking work in space travel.
  • Katherine was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom at the age of 97.
  • Her story was highlighted in the book and successful movie "Hidden Figures".

"Like what you do, and then you will do your best."
